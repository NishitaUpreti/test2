A 5.25 kW solar system for Suvidha Housing Society would significantly reduce electricity costs, provide clean energy, and lower the community’s carbon footprint. With proper installation and maintenance, it can produce 9,581 kWh annually, offering long-term sustainability.

Solar Power System Capacity:

  • System Size: 5.25 kW.
  • Designed to power common areas, street lighting, and reduce electricity bills.

Components of the Solar System:

  • Panels: 15 panels (~350 W each).
  • Inverter: 5-5.5 kW string or microinverter. Optional battery storage for backup.

Energy Production:

  • Daily Output: 26.25 kWh (based on 5 hours of sunlight).
  • Annual Output: 9,581 kWh/year under optimal conditions.

Power Generation Per Year:

  • Annual Output: Around 9,581 kWh per year.
  • This would offset a significant portion of the society's energy needs.

System Installation Considerations:

  • Requires 24-30 m² of roof space with minimal shading.
  • Panels should be oriented south with a 15-20° tilt for optimal sunlight exposure.

Cost Estimation:

  • Estimated cost: ₹2,36,250 for the system.
  • Additional costs may include permits and grid connectivity.

Government Incentives:

  • Up to 30% subsidy available from the government.
  • Net metering allows for credit on the electricity bill for excess energy.

Maintenance and Lifespan:

  • Lifespan: 25-30 years with gradual efficiency loss (0.5% annually).
  • Regular cleaning and system checks are required for optimal performance.

Environmental Impact:

  • Reduces approximately 5,000 kg of CO₂ emissions annually.
  • Helps reduce reliance on fossil fuels and promotes sustainable energy.
