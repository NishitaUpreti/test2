The 2.67 MW Solar Plant at Cochin International Airport is a forward-thinking initiative that integrates renewable energy with innovative infrastructure (solar carports). It not only helps reduce the airport’s carbon footprint but also ensures long-term energy savings. This project is part of CIAL’s commitment to sustainability and energy self-sufficiency, reinforcing its reputation as a pioneer in green aviation and serving as a model for other airports and institutions aiming to reduce their environmental impact.

  • Project Overview:The project involves the installation of a 2.67 MW (megawatt) solar power plant in the form of a solar carport at Cochin International Airport (CIAL) in Kerala, India.
    A solar carport is a structure that combines parking spaces with solar panels, providing both covered parking and generating renewable energy
  • Energy Generation & Use:The 2.67 MW capacity is sufficient to power a large portion of the airport's energy needs, reducing its reliance on the grid.
    The generated solar energy is used for the airport's operational requirements, including lighting, air conditioning, and other systems, reducing the overall electricity costs and ensuring sustainability in airport operations.
    • The 2.67 MW solar plant generates approximately 3.9 million kWh (kilowatt-hours) of electricity annually.
  • Environmental Impact:The project significantly reduces the airport's carbon footprint and supports India's renewable energy goals.
    By utilizing solar power, CIAL contributes to India’s commitment to a greener and more sustainable future.The solar installation is expected to offset approximately 3,300 tons of CO₂ emissions per year.
