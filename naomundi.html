As part of Tata Steel's commitment to sustainability, a 3 MW Solar PV Power Plant was commissioned by Tata Power Solar at the Noamundi Iron Ore Mine, marking a major milestone as the first solar power plant at an iron ore mine in India. This innovative project, established through collaboration among Tata Steel, Tata Power Solar, and Tata Power Trading Company, supports Tata Steel's goal to reduce its carbon footprint. The plant will significantly contribute to environmental conservation by reducing CO₂ emissions by approximately 3,000 tonnes per year. This project also highlights Tata Steel's support for global climate action, aligning with the goals of the UN Climate Change Conference Paris 2015 (COP 21).

Project Overview

  • Location: Noamundi Iron Ore Mine, covering 19 acres of reclaimed mining land.
  • Capacity: 3 MW solar PV power.
  • Technology: Solar PV modules that convert solar radiation into electricity, which is then converted to AC power and fed into the utility grid through a net metering system.
  • Synergy: A collaborative effort among Tata Steel, Tata Power Solar, and Tata Power Trading Company, demonstrating a unified approach to sustainable energy solutions.
  • Special Features: The system is designed to prioritize solar power and only uses grid or diesel generator (DG) power to make up any shortfall, ensuring green energy usage whenever possible.

Benefits

  • Environmental Impact: The facility prioritizes green energy, significantly lowering dependency on grid and DG power.
  • Carbon Emission Reduction: Offsets 3,000 tonnes of CO₂ annually.
  • Eco-friendly Site Management: The reclamation of land for solar generation supports biodiversity, with planned horticulture reducing dust and preserving topsoil.
  • Potential for Expansion: The site has potential for generating up to 4.5 MW of solar power, which could support future expansions of renewable energy capacity.

The 3 MW Solar PV Power Plant at Noamundi Iron Ore Mine is a landmark achievement in India's mining sector. By utilizing solar energy in a challenging terrain, Tata Steel and Tata Power Solar have created a model for sustainable power in industrial settings. This project aligns with Tata Steel's commitment to renewable energy and demonstrates how industry can actively contribute to climate action through innovative technologies and partnerships. As a testament to the potential of clean energy, this installation paves the way for broader adoption of solar energy in India's resource-intensive sectors, fostering a future powered by sustainable practice.
