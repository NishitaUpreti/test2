The Agency for Non-Conventional Energy and Rural Technology (ANERT), along with the Government of India and Kerala, launched a solar power program to electrify rural and suburban areas in Kerala. Tata Power Solar was selected as one of the key vendors for this initiative due to its proven expertise and reliability. The program aimed to install over 7,700 solar systems, with Tata Power Solar receiving the largest order based on customer trust in the Tata brand and the company's quality of service.

Key Highlights:

  • 10.8 MW Distributed Rooftop Systems: The project involved supplying, installing, and commissioning solar systems across 7,750+ locations in Kerala. These systems ranged from small off-grid solutions (1-5 kW) to larger grid-connected systems (2-100 kW), including installations in remote and hard-to-reach areas.
  • Customized Designs for Diverse Roofs: Solar installations had to be tailored to various roof types, from flat and sloping roofs to those shaded by coconut trees. Tata Power Solar engineers customized designs to ensure optimum efficiency for each location.
  • Resilience to High Winds Resilience: Given Kerala's coastal location, the systems were designed to withstand extreme weather conditions, including wind speeds up to 160 km/h.

Impact:

  • Energy Savings: The solar power systems reduced dependency on the local electricity grid (DISCOM), helping the state lower its load on the distribution system. With 300 sunny days a year, these systems met the energy needs of families.
  • Employment Generation: The project created job opportunities for local residents, especially in installation and system maintenance, fostering skill development and training within the community.
  • Improved Quality of Life: The solar installations benefited many elderly residents, providing them with reliable electricity for lighting, fans, TV, and communication devices. This allowed them to stay connected with their families, whether locally or internationally.

In summary, the program successfully harnessed solar energy to improve the lives of rural families in Kerala, generating employment, reducing energy costs, and enhancing connectivity and independence for residents.
