The 300 MW Solar Project in Pavagada, Karnataka, is one of the key installations within the Pavagada Solar Park, which has a total capacity of 2,050 MW. Located in the Tumkur district, this project represents one of the largest and most ambitious solar energy initiatives in India, aimed at accelerating the country’s transition to renewable energy.

  • Installed Capacity: 300 MW of solar power.
  • Annual Energy Generation: The project generates about 450 million kWh annually, enough to power approximately 150,000 households.
  • Environmental Impact: The 300 MW solar plant helps reduce 300,000 tons of CO₂ emissions every year, significantly contributing to India’s environmental sustainability efforts.
  • Land Area: The project covers 1,500 acres within the 13,000-acre Pavagada Solar Park, a vast area optimized for large-scale solar power generation.
  • Investment: The total investment for the 300 MW project is estimated to be between INR 1,500 - 1,800 crore (around USD 200-230 million).
  • Commissioning: The project was completed in phases and fully commissioned in 2018, with construction starting in 2016.
  • Grid Connectivity: The solar plant is connected to the 220 kV transmission network, allowing for seamless integration with the Indian national grid, contributing to the grid’s renewable energy capacity.

  • Renewable Energy Generation:By increasing renewable energy capacity, the project reduces dependency on traditional fossil fuels and enhances India’s energy security.
